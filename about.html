About Us

A Journey of Hope and Empowerment

In May 2024, fresh out of high school, I stumbled upon something that changed my perspective forever. It was a simple essay question on my younger sibling's exam paper: “The Importance of Studying History.” To many, this might seem straightforward, yet he had struggled to answer it. When I asked him why, his response revealed something much deeper than academic weakness—it pointed to an education system that leaves students unprepared to express their knowledge effectively.

That moment stirred something in me. I couldn’t sit back knowing that so many capable students were being held back, not by their abilities, but by the lack of proper guidance and resources. That’s when the idea for WeAllCan Africa was born. Two months later, with a team of 17 young and driven volunteers, I set out to change the narrative.

Our founder reflecting on the journey.

Building a Movement

WeAllCan Africa is more than a project—it’s a mission to uplift and empower secondary school students in underserved communities. Every Saturday, we visit government schools, offering free training sessions to candidate classes. We teach them how to craft exemplary essays, overcome examination anxiety, and manage the precious three hours of their exam time effectively.

In just two months, we’ve visited five schools and interacted with more than 800 students. Watching their confidence grow and seeing over 70% improve academically has been one of the most fulfilling experiences of my life. These aren’t just statistics—they’re stories of young people realizing their potential, stories of dreams reignited.

My Role and My Why

As the founder and leader of this initiative, I am deeply involved in every aspect—from planning sessions to delivering them, and from mentoring my team to engaging directly with the students. My passion for leadership stems from my own journey, growing up in a farming family with limited means but endless hope. The support I received from The School of St. Jude shaped me, and I feel a deep responsibility to give back.

My time as the school food prefect, overseeing meal provisions for over 800 students, taught me the importance of responsibility, organization, and leading by example. Basketball, on the other hand, instilled in me the discipline, teamwork, and determination to strive for excellence. These lessons now guide me as I lead WeAllCan Africa, ensuring that every student and volunteer feels seen, valued, and capable of greatness.

Our dedicated team of volunteers.

Our Aspirations

This initiative is only the beginning. My aspirations extend far beyond this moment—I dream of driving social change through entrepreneurship, business management, and digital literacy. I believe these fields hold the keys to creating sustainable solutions for the systemic challenges my community faces.

As WeAllCan Africa grows, we plan to expand our offerings to include public speaking, debate, and leadership development. We want to equip students not only to excel academically but also to become confident, critical thinkers who can lead their communities to brighter futures.

A Personal Reflection

Every time I stand in front of a classroom filled with eager, determined faces, I am reminded of why I started this journey. It’s not just about helping students pass exams—it’s about showing them that they matter, that their dreams are valid, and that they are capable of achieving anything.

Together with my incredible team, I am committed to creating a ripple effect of hope and empowerment. We believe that change begins with one person daring to take the first step, and that we all can play a part in building a better future.
